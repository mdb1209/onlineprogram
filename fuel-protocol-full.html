02 / Your Numbers

THE MACRO PROTOCOL.

Most calculators spit out a number and walk away. This one teaches you what the numbers mean and why they're yours specifically. Adjust, recalculate, and read the explanations. Bring this in to your Day 0 session and we'll fine-tune in person.

Build Your Plan

Fill in your details. We'll calculate your BMR, TDEE, calorie target, and macro split, then explain every line in plain English.

Your personalized numbers will appear here.

How These Numbers Were Built

We use the Mifflin-St Jeor equation for BMR (your resting metabolism), multiply by your activity factor for TDEE (total daily energy expenditure), add training calories, then apply your goal adjustment. Protein is set by lean body mass (or weight if BF unknown) at 0.8 - 1.0g per pound. Fat is set as a percent of calories based on hormonal needs (women generally need more fat). Carbs fill the rest. This is the same framework used by registered dietitians and sports nutrition coaches - we just apply it specifically to you.

05 / Survival Mode

THE FIRST
SEVEN DAYS.

Week one is the hardest week. Your body is recalibrating, your habits are screaming, and your willpower is finite. Here's exactly what to do each day so you don't have to think.

Day 01
Measure & Stock

In-person measurement session. Photograph the empty fridge before you shop. Buy the full grocery list. Drink 80oz water today.

Day 02
Hit Protein Only

Don't worry about anything except hitting your protein target today. Carbs and fats can be approximate. Protein is the foundation.

Day 03
The Hunger Wave

This is when most people quit. Drink 16oz water, eat a high-protein snack, and walk for 10 minutes. The wave passes in 20 minutes.

Day 04
Carb Day

Time your carbs around your training session. You should feel stronger in the gym today than day one. That's the protocol working.

Day 05
The Social Test

Friday. Restaurant. Drinks. Order first. Protein + 2 vegetables + small starch. One drink, sparkling water in between. You're allowed to enjoy your life.

Day 06
Recovery

Rest day from training. Don't compensate by eating less. Eat to your maintenance number. Sleep 8 hours. Walk after dinner.

Day 07
Reset & Prep

Sunday. 30 minutes of meal prep. Cook the week's protein in bulk. Wash and chop vegetables. Portion snacks. Set up week two for autopilot.

If You're Hungry
  • Drink 16 oz of water first
  • Wait 15 minutes
  • If still hungry, eat protein + fiber (eggs + spinach, cottage cheese + berries)
  • Real hunger doesn't go away. Cravings do.
If You're Tired
  • Check sleep first - aim for 7+ hours
  • Add 1 cup more carbs around training
  • Add electrolytes (LMNT, salt + lemon water)
  • Week 1 fatigue is normal and passes by day 10
If You Slip
  • Don't "make up for it" by skipping the next meal
  • Don't restart Monday - resume the next bite
  • One meal off plan is a meal. Three is a pattern.
  • Text your coach. We troubleshoot together.
06 / Stick the Landing

HOW TO ACTUALLY
FOLLOW THROUGH.

Adherence is the single biggest predictor of results. Not the perfect plan. Not the fanciest macros. The plan you follow most days for 90 days. Here's the system.

The Five Rules

  1. Protein at every meal. If you only do one thing, do this. Aim for 30-50g per meal. It anchors satiety, training, and hormonal balance.
  2. Plate before you sit. Every plate gets a visual: ½ vegetables, ¼ protein, ¼ starchy carb, thumb of fat. You don't need to weigh every meal forever.
  3. The 80/20 rule. 80% of meals on protocol, 20% flexible. Math it out: 21 meals/week, 4 are negotiable. That includes wine, a slice of birthday cake, the kids' fries.
  4. Hydrate before you eat. 16oz water 20 minutes before each meal. Hunger and thirst share the same signal. Most "hunger" at 3pm is dehydration.
  5. Sleep is nutrition. Under 6 hours of sleep raises cortisol, ghrelin, and cravings. The fastest way to crush week 2 is to sleep 7+ hours every night of week 1.

The Tracking Setup

You don't have to track forever. Track for the first 30 days so you learn portions, then dial back to "spot checking" once intuition kicks in.

  • App: MacroFactor (paid, smartest) or MyFitnessPal (free)
  • Scale: Cheap kitchen scale, $15. Weigh meat raw, oils always.
  • Body weight: Same time, same conditions, 3x per week. Take the average.
  • Photos: Same lighting, same outfit, every Sunday morning. Front, side, back.
  • Energy/sleep: 1-10 score in your phone notes. We'll review at check-ins.

07 / Built-In Accountability

THREE TIMES
FACE TO FACE.

Apps don't change bodies. People do. Your $199 includes three in-person measurement sessions where we look at the data together and adjust the plan.

Day 0 · Onboarding
Baseline + Plan Build
45 minutes. We measure body weight, waist, hips, chest, thigh, and arm. Take front/side/back photos. Review your macro calculator output and adjust based on training history, lifestyle, and goals. You walk out with your full 90-day protocol printed.
Day 30 · First Adjust
Recalibrate
30 minutes. Same measurements. Compare to baseline. If progress is on track, we maintain. If too aggressive (energy crashing, sleep suffering, training declining) we pull calories up. If stalled, we audit adherence first, then adjust calories. You walk out with updated targets.
Day 90 · Final Read
Results + Maintenance Map
45 minutes. Final measurements and photos. Side-by-side comparison to Day 0. We build your "maintenance protocol" - the new calorie and macro targets for life after the program, and the habits that proved most valuable for you specifically.
What We Measure
  • Body weight (same scale, same time of morning)
  • Circumference: waist (at navel), hips (widest), chest, mid-thigh, mid-bicep
  • Optional: body fat % via skinfold or bioimpedance
  • Progress photos (front, side, back, same lighting)
  • Strength benchmarks at CultureFit (deadlift, squat, bench / press)
  • Subjective markers: energy, sleep, cravings, mood
What "Adjustment" Looks Like
  • Lost weight too fast (more than 1% body weight per week)? +150-200 calories
  • Stalled for 2+ weeks with high adherence? -100-150 calories or +500 daily steps
  • Strength dropping? Add 25-50g carbs around training
  • Sleep suffering? Add carbs to dinner, push final meal earlier
  • Cycle changes / period missed (female)? We pull calories up immediately
  • Plateau with high stress? We address sleep and cortisol before food
